# Hero Image Provenance Notes

Per-entry notes on hero image source, rights status, and any concerns
relevant to future commercial reproduction (e.g. coffee-table book).

Most entries are not listed here — clean public-domain or fair-use cases
(government documents, court filings, BIPs, the catalog's own SVG plates,
Wikipedia images, etc.) are assumed clean and need no notes.

This document is internal editorial reference, not displayed publicly.

Status legend:
- ✓ Clean — public domain, CC-licensed, or the catalog's own work
- ◐ Standard fair use — partial reproduction with commentary, widely
  precedented in existing literature
- ⚠️ Needs attention — flag for explicit permission or substitution
  before commercial reproduction
- ? Unknown — image is in use but provenance hasn't been documented

When the book project goes serious: walk every entry's hero image, log it
here, work through ⚠️ flags one by one with a publishing lawyer.

## Foundational documents

### btc-2008-001-the-whitepaper
**Image:** screenshot of "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System" page 1
**Source:** bitcoin.org PDF (or the version archived at the Satoshi Nakamoto Institute)
**Status:** ◐ Standard fair use
**Notes:** The Bitcoin whitepaper has a contested copyright history. Released
publicly Oct 2008 with no formal license; bitcoin.org distributed it under
MIT-style terms for years. Craig Wright sent legal threats to bitcoin.org in
2021 claiming copyright; UK courts (COPA v. Wright, March 2024) ruled he is
not Satoshi and was a serial forger of evidence. Practical reality: the actual
copyright holder (Satoshi) is anonymous and unreachable, so no party can grant
or refuse permission. Reproducing the first page with attribution and editorial
commentary is precedented across every serious bitcoin book. Strong fair-use
case: transformative commentary, partial reproduction (1 of 9 pages), educational
purpose. Low practical risk.

### cypherpunk-manifesto
**Image:** screenshot of "A Cypherpunk's Manifesto" from Satoshi Nakamoto Institute archive
**Source:** nakamotoinstitute.org/library/cypherpunk-manifesto/
**Author:** Eric Hughes, March 9, 1993, originally posted to cypherpunks mailing list
**Status:** ◐ Standard fair use; permission likely available if requested
**Notes:** Eric Hughes is alive, reachable, public figure in cryptography
community. For a book project, send a permission request — likely granted readily
for a serious editorial work. Until then, fair use applies as with the whitepaper:
transformative commentary, partial reproduction, educational purpose.

### btc-2016-001-lightning-whitepaper
**Image:** screenshot of Lightning Network whitepaper title page
**Source:** lightning.network/lightning-network-paper.pdf
**Authors:** Joseph Poon and Thaddeus Dryja, January 2016
**Status:** ◐ Standard fair use; permission readily obtainable
**Notes:** Both authors alive, reachable, active in bitcoin community. Permission
request would be a formality for a book project.

### btc-2018-001-the-bitcoin-standard
**Image:** book cover, Saifedean Ammous, *The Bitcoin Standard* (Wiley, 2018)
**Status:** ⚠️ Needs attention
**Notes:** Wiley owns the cover design. Book covers are a standard fair-use case
when reproduced as part of editorial commentary about the book itself, but for
a printed book, publishers typically request permission from the publisher of
the cited work as a courtesy. Easy clearance. Saifedean is also reachable.

## Maintenance protocol

- Add an entry here when:
  1. Adding a new entry whose hero image has any rights question
  2. Discovering rights information about an existing entry's image
  3. Substituting an image (note both old and new sources)

- Audit pass before any book project goes serious:
  walk every entry, confirm provenance for ✓ entries, resolve ⚠️ entries
  with permission letters or substitutions, document ? entries.
